Methodology

From Signals to Decisions

A transparent, data‑driven framework that models borrower risk dynamically using interpretable components and ODE‑based evolution.

RK45
Solver Engine
Interpretable
Glass‑box modelling

State Equation

$$\Psi_t = \alpha\,\lVert X_t \rVert + \beta\,\sigma_t + \gamma\,\dot R_t + \delta\,\Phi_t + \varepsilon\,P_t + \zeta\,\Omega_t + \eta\,F_t$$

Latent Preferences \((P_t)\)

$$P_t = \frac{1}{1 + e^{-(\beta_0 + \beta_1\,\text{Flag}+\cdots)}}$$

  • Categorized expenditure from merchant codes
  • Card/QR spending patterns
  • FMCG purchase patterns

Debt Burden \((\lVert X_t \rVert)\)

$$\lVert X_t \rVert = \frac{\sum_i E_i}{\text{Monthly Income}}$$

  • Loan contracts, bureau snapshots
  • EMI detection from bank statements
  • Telecom postpaid bills

Foresight Score \((F_t)\)

$$\frac{dF_t}{dt} = \psi_1 C_t - \psi_2 U_t - \lambda F_t$$

  • FD top‑ups, recurring deposits
  • Advance utility payments
  • Insurance premium regularity

Peer Contagion \((\Phi_t)\)

$$\frac{d\Phi_t}{dt} = \nu\,(\overline{\Phi} - \Phi_t)$$

  • Geo‑aggregates at PIN‑code level
  • MFI default rates, mobility, MSME trends
  • Distress keywords; payday loan spikes

Financial Fatigue \((\Omega_t)\)

$$\frac{d\Omega_t}{dt} = \theta_1\,\#\text{Shocks} - \theta_2\,\frac{1}{t_r}$$

  • Consented UPI & transactional SMS
  • Macro and local shock indices

Recovery Rate \((\dot R_t)\)

$$\frac{dR_t}{dt} = -\lambda R_t + \omega S_t$$

  • Repayment histories, post‑arrears recharges
  • Utility bill catch‑ups; co‑op credit logs

Income Volatility \((\sigma_t)\)

$$\frac{d\sigma_t}{dt} = \kappa\,(\lvert\Delta I_{t-1}\rvert - \sigma_t)$$

  • Payroll deposits, GST returns
  • Seasonal wage patterns, bank credits

Forecasting Engine

Calibration: Data over Assumptions

  1. Discretize continuous ODEs to discrete time.
  2. Regress to learn best‑fit parameters from history.

$$\sigma_t - \sigma_{t-1} \approx \kappa\,(\lvert\Delta I_{t-1}\rvert - \sigma_{t-1})$$

Adaptive RK45 step‑size: large steps in stable periods, finer during shocks.

Elastic Net Training

$$\hat{\beta} = \arg\min_{\beta}\,\Big(\lVert y - X\beta \rVert_2^2 + \lambda_1\lVert\beta\rVert_1 + \lambda_2\lVert\beta\rVert_2^2\Big)$$

  • Balances sparsity (L1) and stability (L2)
  • Robust when predictors are correlated
  • Interpretable and production‑friendly
